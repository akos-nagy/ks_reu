	What follows is a computation of the recursion for $S_{n+1,\uk}$ when the initial conditions are `1D'.  That is, all $S_{0,\uk}$ are zero except possibly $S_{0,\underline0}$ and $S_{0,(1,0)}$.  We suppress the second coordinate in the indicies (that is, $S_{0,(a,b)}=S_{0,a}$).  

	%Notice that \[S_{n,n+1}=\frac{(n+1)^n}{n!}R_{0,1}^{n+1}.\]
	Notice that
	\begin{align*}
		S_{n,2}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^2\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^2n!},\\
		S_{n,3}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^3\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^4n!}(-2^n\frac3{2!}+3^n),\\
		S_{n,4}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^4\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^6n!}(3^n\frac4{3!}+4^n\frac48-5^n\frac42+6^n),\\
		S_{n,5}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^5\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^8n!}(-4^n\frac5{4!}-6^n\frac5{12}+7^n\frac56+8^n\frac54-9^n\frac52+10^n),\\
		S_{n,6}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^6\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^{10}n!}(5^n\frac6{5!}+8^n\frac6{48}-9^n\frac6{36}-11^n\frac66+12^n\frac68+13^n\frac6{8/3}-14^n\frac62+15^n),\\
		S_{n,7}&=\frac{S_{0,1}^7\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^{12}n!}(-6^n\frac7{6!}-10^n\frac7{240}+11^n\frac7{120}-12^n\frac7{144}+14^n\frac7{24}-15^n\frac7{72}+16^n\frac7{24}-17^n\frac74+18^n\frac7{24}+19^n\frac72-20^n\frac72+21^n)
	\end{align*}
	These identities have been verified for $n$ up to 50.  The general form seems to be
	\[S_{n,i}=\frac{S_{0,1}^i\pi^{2n}2^{3n}}{(2\pi)^{2i-2}n!}\sum_{j=i-1}^{\binom i2}c_{n,i,j}j^n\]
